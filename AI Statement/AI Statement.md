## What I Used AI For

<!-- Describe the areas where you used AI assistance. Examples below — edit to match your actual use. -->

- **Game Concept & Design:** Used AI to brainstorm initial horror puzzle mechanics, room layout ideas, and enemy behaviour patterns.
- **User Stories & Documentation:** Used AI to help structure and word User Stories and acceptance criteria.
- **Code Assistance:** Used AI to suggest Unity C# scripts for specific systems (e.g., NavMesh enemy patrol, interaction prompts).
- **Writing & Descriptions:** Used AI to draft in-game clue text and environmental storytelling notes.

---

## How I Adjusted and Evaluated AI Output

<!-- Describe how you critically reviewed and modified what the AI gave you. This is important — show that YOU were in control. -->

AI outputs were never used directly without review. My adjustment process typically involved:

1. **Evaluating relevance** — Checking whether the AI's suggestion actually fit the tone and mechanics of *Subject 17*.
2. **Modifying structure** — Restructuring AI-generated content to match my own design vision (e.g., changing puzzle logic, rebalancing enemy detection ranges).
3. **Rejecting unsuitable outputs** — Some suggestions were discarded entirely as they conflicted with the intended player experience of tension and vulnerability.
4. **Combining with my own decisions** — Final creative and technical decisions were always made by me, with AI serving as a sounding board rather than an author.
